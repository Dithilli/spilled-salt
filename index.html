A Closer Reckoning — Egypt

—

In Egypt, salt is two opposed substances at once. Natron — netjry, the "divine salt" — preserves the blessed dead and purifies the temple. But common sea-salt is the very foam of Typhon, which the priests shunned. So Egypt's whole verdict turns on a single question: what did you actually spill? (This refines Set and Isis & Osiris from the world tally above.)

A Closer Reckoning — Mesopotamia

—

Here salt is overwhelmingly holy. In the great anti-witchcraft rite of Maqlû, salt is addressed as a power in its own right — "O Salt, created in a pure place" — and begged to dissolve any sorcery laid upon the speaker. So your spill is, by accident, a small spell-breaking working. The land of the two rivers looks kindly on it.

No single act satisfies both columns — hence the protocol

The Reconciling Protocol

  1. At once Cast one pinch over the left shoulder.

    Settles the Western omen and strikes the Devil who waits there. Costs you almost nothing from the heap.

  2. Through the night Leave the main spill across the threshold.

    Holds the counting-trap, the repulsion-wall, and the Southeast Asian weapon at full power while the dark hours run.

  3. At first light Rake the spent salt into a single cone.

    Converts the used ward into a proper Shinto morijio — retroactively squaring the purification column and closing the books clean.
